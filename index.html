Our brand

At City Index, we celebrate the craft of our traders. We celebrate their ability to push boundaries, explore new markets and master new skills with a sense of quiet confidence. And we appreciate their determination not to shy away from challenges, but embrace them. Ultimately, we are guided by a core belief – that we only succeed when our traders succeed.

So no matter where you are on your trading journey, City Index is here to put you in the driver’s seat of your own success. We understand that although you can never predict the markets, with discipline and insight and a dependable platform, you can achieve great things.

So just like our traders, we’ve spent over 35 years navigating volatile markets and enhancing our technology and our service singularly focused on helping our clients achieve – for every move they make.

The Logo

1

Our logo is a straight forward and accessible wordmarque with rounded edges to provide a friendlier tone to the capital letters.

Use the logo artwork file when creating communications. The Logo should only appear using the system colour palette.

Clear space

To make sure our logo always remains prominent and legible, we have defined an area of clear space equivalent to the height of our wordmarques X. This area should be kept free from text, busy patterns and backgrounds, other logos or other visual distractions.

Please note that this page indicates the absolute minimum clear space. Where possible, more should be used.

Logo positioning

In most layouts, the logo should be aligned to the margin corners. Preference on corner should be decided by white space available in the design.

In cases where the logo is the main focus of the page it may be centred.

Minimum scale

As well as using clear space, we can also keep our logo legible by ensuring it is never reproduced smaller than the minimum sizes outlined opposite.

Note that these are absolute minimum sizes, and that it is recommended to use the logos bigger.

Recommended scale

On every application, we must ensure that the logo strikes a balance on scale. It must be large enough to clearly brand the application, but subtle enough to avoid undermining our trusted aesthetic.

Exception to the rule is for social media in which the CI element is recommended.

Please note that these guidelines are for the most common formats only. For extreme aspect ratios or small formats, exercise your best judgement.

Partnerships

Aligning partnership logos should follow the clear space rules. The logos should be separated by a round cap keyline in light grey, which is 2 px on screen, or 1 pt in print.

Sub lines and associations

Our logo can support sub lines and associations. The line must be locked up, right aligned using our typeface Aeonik regular. The sub line copy should be typeset to under 1⁄4 the X height of the logo.

Animation

When we have the opportunity to animate the logo to reveal it there are some guardrails to deliver a ownable and consistent animated personality.

Movement
Our logo reveal is compromised of a single 'draw-in' animation revealing all letters consistently by following the geometric forms simple shapes.

All letters animate at the same pace with some starting slightly later than others to allow for a consistent completion together. This allows no single letter form to dominate or catch the eye on completion.

Secondary logo

Our secondary logo provides a handy shorter iteration of the logo. The stacked offset form provides a more dynamic option when required.

It follows the same logic in application as the core logo. It should not be used in conjunction with sub lines or associations.

The CI element

The CI element is a supporting device to the core logo. It is simple, distinct reinforces CI as the brand acronym. It is designed to lock up with sub brands.

The predominant use is within App icons and mobile experiences.

Use the CI element artwork file when creating communications. The CI element should predominantly appear using the system colour palette. On rare occasion it can use the omni colour shapes palette when the system colour palette is not suitable.

Social and app icon

App icons and social media profile images are exceptions to the rule that the logo takes precedent. Due to these formats’ small scale and 1:1 aspect ratio, we use the CI element alone to maximise impact and brand recognition.

Social and app icon Alternative

If an alternative option for social and app icon is required we can apply the omni 1a of the colour shapes palette within the circle. See Colour gradients for application.

Sub brands

Sub brands such as Premium, Pro and Investor can have supporting CI element lockups to give greater distinction and credibility. They are created by combining the CI element with our typeface Aeonik regular.

Examples of these have been designed and artworked, so use the appropriate artworked file for ease of application but If new sub brands need to be created clear space between the word and the CI element should be defined by the distance between the ‘I’ and the diameter of the outer circle.

CI element in use

Logo tips. Do's and do not's

Use the supplied artwork file. Do not recreate it.

Apply the system colour palette to the logo in solid not outline.

Only use the omni colour shapes palette as an alternative of the system colour palette for the CI element if the system palette is not suitable. This should be rare occasions.

Do not rotate, flip, reverse or use the logo on It's side.

Do not distort, crop, change the transparency or apply effects.

The Typeface

2

Our typeface is Aeonik by CoType. A timeless, strong and rigid geometric sans serif providing a strong and stable backbone to our visual language.

For Instances where we’re unable to use Aeonik (for instance in email newsletters) we should use Poppins – a free Google Font by Jonny Pinhorn.

Aa

Setting type

Weight
For elegance and clarity, we use the regular weight of Aeonik set in sentence case. Bold weight may be used sparingly for emphasis.

Leading
Headings: 110% of the type size. Body Copy: 120–130% of the type size.

Tracking and Kerning
Headings: Optical kerning with 0 tracking.
Body Copy: Metric kerning with 0 tracking

Where possible, large text (>50pt) should be kerned manually.

Line Length
For the easiest reading, try to keep lines to an average of between 45 and 90 characters.

Paragraph Rules
If you find that it helps to break up your text, use a 1px / 0.5pt keyline spanning the column above your text.
